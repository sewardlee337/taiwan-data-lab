The Gist


In a world that is increasingly dominated by data-driven technologies and decision-making, that which is not quantified is invisible. Due to the role that data increasingly plays in both civic participation and technological growth, governments around the world are increasingly providing public access to data for researchers, technologists, and civic hackers. While Taiwan has taken steps to join the Open Data bandwagon, more work needs to be done to make its data readily accessible and easy to consume.

Economic, political, and demographic data relating to Taiwan are conspicuously lacking or absent from the repositories of international organizations such as the United Nations, World Bank, and International Monetary Fund. The problem is exacerbated by the fact that official sources of Taiwan data are not particularly user-friendly — especially for international researchers who cannot read Traditional Chinese. A 2014 article in CommonWealth Magazine (天下雜誌) has profiled the latter issue, further noting that what data are publicly available are often offered in Microsoft Word and PDF files.

Taiwan Data Lab is an effort to make Taiwan data accessible and useful to analysts and scholars around the world. Built with open source technologies, Taiwan Data Lab provides curated, machine-readable datasets and interactive dashboards for researchers who want to discover Taiwan.
